# Honda F-Series

## Overview
The Honda F-series is a 2.0–2.3 liter inline-4 produced from 1989 to 2002 in the Honda Accord, Prelude, and Odyssey. Three configurations: F20 (2.0L, 1989–1991), F22 (2.2L, 1990–1997), and F23 (2.3L, 1998–2002). All are SOHC 16-valve except the F22B1/B6 which have VTEC. The F22B1 (1994–1997 Accord EX) is notable as Honda's first VTEC engine in a mainstream family sedan. The F-series is understressed, smooth, and remarkably reliable when maintained. Many F22 engines exceed 300,000 miles with basic care.

## Known Issues
- **Balance shaft seal leak (F22A, F22B)** — The balance shaft seal behind the timing belt cover weeps oil. Oil-soaked timing belt accelerates wear. Fix: replace seal whenever timing belt is serviced.
- **Timing belt idler bearing failure** — Idler bearing whines by 100k miles. Non-interference on most F-series (verify year). Replace with belt.
- **VTEC solenoid gasket leak (F22B1, F22B6)** — VTEC solenoid on top of valve cover has a small gasket that hardens. Oil drips onto alternator and exhaust.
- **Distributor O-ring leak** — Same as D-series: O-ring hardens, oil leaks down distributor shaft onto exhaust. Burning oil smell.
- **Coolant passage corrosion (F22A)** — Early F22A engines had cast iron coolant passages that corrode internally. Flaking rust blocks heater core. Periodic coolant changes prevent this.
- **Automatic transmission failure (F22 + 4-speed auto)** — The PX4B/MPZA transmissions behind F-series engines are prone to 2nd gear clutch pack failure. Symptoms: flare on 1→2 shift. Honda ATF-Z1 required; Dexron causes rapid failure.
- **Motor mount failure (F22 in Accord)** — The front and rear hydraulic mounts rupture, causing vibration at idle and engine rocking on acceleration. Replace with urethane mounts or quality OEM.
- **EGR valve clogging** — Carbon buildup in EGR valve pintle and passages. Sets P0401. Remove valve, clean pintle and passages with carb cleaner.
- **O2 sensor slow response (high mileage)** — Post-cat O2 sensor becomes sluggish. P0133/P0153. Replace with Denso or NTK.

## Common Procedures

### Balance Shaft Seal Replacement
1. Remove timing belt covers, crank pulley (181 ft-lb), and timing belt.
2. The balance shaft is the smaller sprocket below the cam sprocket.
3. Remove balance shaft sprocket bolt. Slide sprocket off.
4. Pry out old seal carefully (don't scratch shaft).
5. Install new seal with seal driver. Lip faces INWARD (toward engine).
6. Reinstall sprocket, timing belt, tensioner. Torque crank pulley to 181 ft-lb.

### VTEC Solenoid Gasket
1. Located on top of valve cover, near driver's side.
2. Remove 3 10mm bolts. Lift solenoid.
3. Replace the small paper gasket. Clean mating surfaces.
4. Reinstall. Torque to 7 ft-lb. Don't overtighten — aluminum threads strip.

### Motor Mount Replacement
1. Front mount: remove center bolt, jack engine slightly (wood block under oil pan).
2. Rear mount: remove bolts from transmission tunnel. May require dropping exhaust slightly.
3. Side mounts: remove through-bolts, lift engine with floor jack.
4. Install new mounts. Lower engine gently. Torque all bolts.

## Special Tools
- Impact gun (crank pulley 181 ft-lb)
- Seal driver set (balance shaft seal)
- Floor jack + wood block (motor mount replacement)
- Honda scan tool or OBD-II scanner (VTEC activation test)

## Common Mistakes
- **Not replacing balance shaft seal with timing belt** — The seal is $5 and 5 minutes extra labor. Skipping it guarantees oil contamination of the new belt.
- **Wrong transmission fluid** — Honda ATF-Z1 only. Dexron or generic ATF destroys Honda automatics.
- **Reusing timing belt tensioner** — Non-interference doesn't mean non-critical. The tensioner fails quietly then the belt jumps.
- **Overtorquing VTEC solenoid** — Those are tiny 10mm bolts into aluminum. 7 ft-lb. Finger-tight plus quarter turn.
- **Using non-Honda coolant** — Type 2 has specific silicate package. Green coolant causes corrosion of the cast iron sleeves in early F22A.
- **Ignoring 1→2 shift flare** — It's not "normal aging." It's clutch pack failure. Early fluid change + friction modifier may save it.
