# Toyota 22RE

## Overview
The Toyota 22RE is a 2.4 liter inline-4 fuel-injected engine produced from 1983 to 2004 in Toyota Pickup trucks, 4Runners, and early Tacomas. It replaced the carbureted 22R with electronic fuel injection, a cast iron block, aluminum head, timing chain, and single overhead cam. Known for extreme longevity — many exceed 300,000 miles with basic maintenance. Timing chain driven from the crank via dual-row chain with plastic guide rails.

## Known Issues
- **Timing chain rattle at 200,000+ miles** — Plastic guide rails crumble with age, chain slaps cover. Sounds like marbles in a tin can at cold start.
- **Cracked exhaust manifold** — Very common. The cast iron manifold develops hairline cracks between ports 1–2 or 3–4. Leak sounds like ticking, may set oxygen sensor codes.
- **EGR system clogging** — EGR passages in intake manifold clog with carbon, causing hesitation, stumble, and failed emissions.
- **Oxygen sensor failure** — Single-wire O2 sensor degrades slowly. Poor economy before codes appear.
- **AFM (Air Flow Meter) drift** — The flapper-door AFM develops worn potentiometer tracks, causing erratic idle and off-throttle stumble.
- **Starter solenoid contacts** — Copper contacts erode; starts require multiple key turns.

## Common Procedures

### Timing Chain Replacement
1. Remove valve cover, inspect guide rail condition. Crumbles = replace all guides + chain + tensioner.
2. Set engine to TDC compression on cylinder #1 (cam sprocket dot at 12 o'clock).
3. Remove harmonic balancer (87 ft-lb, hold with strap wrench or impact socket).
4. Remove timing cover. NOTE: Cover is two-piece upper/lower; upper is behind water pump.
5. Remove chain. Inspect crank sprocket for hooked teeth.
6. Install new chain with colored links aligned to dots on sprockets.
7. Install new guide rails (both sides) and tensioner.
8. Reassemble with fresh RTV on timing cover joints.
9. Torque harmonic balancer to 87 ft-lb. Use new bolt if stretched.

### Exhaust Manifold Replacement
1. Soak manifold nuts in PB Blaster for 24 hours before attempting. Broken studs are common.
2. Remove EGR tube, oxygen sensor, heat shield.
3. Remove nuts in crisscross pattern working from outside in.
4. If stud snaps: extract with left-hand drill + easy-out, or weld nut on.
5. Clean head surface with Scotch-Brite pad. Do NOT gouge.
6. Apply nickel anti-seize to new studs, torque to 33 ft-lb.
7. Install new gasket (dry — graphite composite, no RTV needed).
8. Retorque after first heat cycle (100 miles).

## Special Tools
- 30 mm socket for harmonic balancer bolt
- Strap wrench or chain wrench to hold balancer
- Crows foot for tight exhaust manifold nut near firewall
- Pipe thread chaser for oxygen sensor bung (if cross-threaded)

## Common Mistakes
- **Forgetting to align chain colored links** — engine will not run, valves may hit pistons if you force it.
- **Using RTV on exhaust manifold gasket** — burns out immediately, creates leak path.
- **Reusing stretched head bolts** — Toyota specifies non-TTY but stretch is visible after removal. Replace for safety.
- **Skipping the timing cover lower half** — Many mechanics leave the lower piece with water pump attached; it shares a seal surface with the upper. Both must be resealed.
