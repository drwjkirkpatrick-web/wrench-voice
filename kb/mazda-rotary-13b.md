# Mazda Rotary 13B

## Overview
The Mazda 13B is a 1.3 liter twin-rotor Wankel engine produced from 1974 to 2012 in the RX-7 (SA, FB, FC, FD) and RX-8 (RENESIS variant). It is NOT a piston engine — it uses triangular rotors orbiting inside epitrochoid housings. No reciprocating mass means 9,000+ RPM redlines and buttery smoothness. However, rotor seals (apex seals) are sacrificial wear items and the engine consumes oil by design (injected for seal lubrication). Two major eras: 13B with peripheral port intake (RX-7 FC) and 13B-REW with sequential twin turbo (RX-7 FD). RX-8 RENESIS used side exhaust ports (no peripheral port) for emissions but lost some power.

## Known Issues
- **Apex seal failure** — The three-corner seals on each rotor wear. Compression drops, hard starting, power loss. Premixed 2-stroke oil in fuel extends life. Hot shutdowns accelerate wear.
- **Side seal / corner seal blow-by** — Compression escapes between side housings, causing erratic idle and oil burning. Requires engine teardown or replacement.
- **Oil metering pump (OMP) failure** — OMP injects oil into combustion chamber for apex seal lubrication. Failure = accelerated seal wear, overheating. Test by checking oil level drop between fills.
- **Cooling system inadequacy** — Rotary engines run hot. Clogged radiators, failed fans, or low coolant cause rapid overheating. Above 220°F sustained = danger zone.
- **Carbon buildup in ports** — Peripheral and side exhaust ports accumulate carbon, restricting flow and raising EGT. Decarbonize with Seafoam or water/methanol injection. Some mechanics perform "Italian tune-up" (high RPM highway runs).
- **RX-8 RENESIS flooding** — Rotary engines flood easily with cold starts and short trips. De-flood procedure requires pulling fuel pump fuse and cranking with throttle wide open.
- **Turbine seal failure (13B-REW)** — Twin turbochargers have ceramic seals that fail with age. Oil leak into compressor or turbine housings.

## Common Procedures

### Apex Seal Replacement (Overview)
1. **WARNING: Major engine overhaul.** Requires engine removal, complete disassembly, and precision measurement.
2. Remove engine from vehicle. Remove intake, exhaust, manifolds, accessories.
3. Remove front cover, oil pump, and eccentric shaft pulley.
4. Split engine at rotor housings. Remove rotors. Inspect rotor housings for chrome flaking or scoring.
5. Install new apex seals, side seals, corner seals, and springs. All must be measured for height uniformity (within 0.001").
6. Reassemble with new rotor housing O-rings and side housing gaskets.
7. Torque eccentric shaft nut to 320 ft-lb with counterhold fixture.
8. Prime oil system before first start. Run at idle until warm, verify no leaks.

### De-Flood Procedure (RX-8 / Flooded Rotary)
1. Remove fuel pump fuse (engine compartment fuse box).
2. Depress accelerator pedal fully.
3. Crank engine for 10–15 seconds. Repeat 3–4 times.
4. Reinstall fuel pump fuse.
5. Start normally. Do NOT touch throttle during start.
6. If still flooded, remove spark plugs, dry/clean, crank with plugs out to expel fuel/oil mist.

## Special Tools
- Rotary compression tester (reads per-chamber, not per-cylinder; 3 readings per rotor)
- Eccentric shaft counterhold tool (essential for 320 ft-lb nut)
- Atkins Rotary apex seal installation fixture (or equivalent)
- Digital caliper measuring to 0.0005" (seal height measurement)
- Vacuum gauge with rotary-specific interpretation chart

## Common Mistakes
- **Treating it like a piston engine** — Rotaries consume oil by design. Low oil = seal death. Check oil every 1,000 miles.
- **Hot shutdown** — Shutting off a hot rotary stops coolant circulation at peak temps. Idle 30 seconds before shutdown.
- **Premixing incorrectly** — Too little oil = seal wear. Too much = carbon fouling. Measure with ratio rite cup.
- **Wrong spark plugs** — NGK RE8C-L (copper, short reach) only. Platinum or long-reach plugs destroy housings.
- **Ignoring compression test** — Standard compression testers don't work. You need a rotary tester that records 3 pulses per rotor face.
