# Honda R-Series

## Overview
The Honda R-series is a 1.8 liter inline-4 produced from 2006 to 2015 in the Honda Civic (8th gen FA/FG, 9th gen FB). Single variant in North America: R18A1 (SOHC 16-valve i-VTEC, 140 hp). The R18 uses a simplified i-VTEC system that switches between low-lift and high-lift cam profiles at 4,500 RPM — less aggressive than the K-series but improves efficiency. The R18 is the spiritual successor to the D-series: simple, understressed, and built for economy rather than performance. It's also one of the last SOHC engines Honda produced.

## Known Issues
- **Valve cover gasket leak** — Oil seeps onto exhaust manifold. Same as every Honda. Replace gasket, 7 ft-lb.
- **Timing chain tensioner rattle (high mileage)** — Tensioner bleeds down overnight. Brief rattle at cold start. Replace before chain skips.
- **VTEC oil pressure switch failure** — The i-VTEC solenoid pressure switch leaks externally. Oil drips onto alternator. Replace switch and clean connector.
- **Throttle body carbon buildup** — Drive-by-wire throttle plate accumulates carbon, causing idle droop and P0505. Clean with throttle body cleaner.
- **Automatic transmission judder (R18 + 5-speed auto)** — The 5-speed automatic has a torque converter that shudders at 35–45 mph. Honda TSB recommends ATF DW-1 fluid change + software update.
- **Engine mount failure (8th gen Civic)** — The front hydraulic mount ruptures by 100k miles. Causes vibration in Drive at idle. Replace with urethane or quality OEM.
- **Coolant temperature sensor drift** — Reads cold when engine is warm. Causes rich running and poor MPG. Replace sensor.
- **O2 sensor heater failure (high mileage)** — Sets P0135. Downstream sensor easier. Bosch or NTK replacement.
- **Crankshaft position sensor failure** — Intermittent no-start when hot. Sensor located at crank pulley.

## Common Procedures

### Timing Chain Tensioner Replacement
1. Remove valve cover. Remove timing chain cover.
2. Requires harmonic balancer removal (181 ft-lb).
3. Remove old tensioner. Prime new one by pushing plunger.
4. Install new tensioner. Release.
5. Hand-turn crank two revolutions. Verify tension.
6. Reassemble with fresh RTV on chain cover.

### Throttle Body Cleaning
1. Remove intake tubing. Disconnect throttle connector.
2. Spray throttle body cleaner on plate and bore.
3. Work plate open gently with finger (do NOT force — drive-by-wire motor is fragile).
4. Wipe with lint-free cloth.
5. Reconnect. Reset throttle learn: key ON, wait 2 minutes without touching pedal.

### VTEC Oil Pressure Switch
1. Located on valve cover, driver's side.
2. Remove 12mm bolt. Disconnect connector.
3. Replace switch with new O-ring.
4. Torque to 7 ft-lb.

## Common Mistakes
- **Reusing timing chain tensioner** — Rattle means replace. Chain skipping = bent valves.
- **Forcing throttle plate** — Drive-by-wire motors strip easily. Gentle finger pressure only.
- **Wrong ATF** — DW-1 only for 5-speed auto. Dexron causes judder.
- **Not resetting throttle learn after cleaning** — Idle will hunt for days until ECU relearns.
- **Ignoring transmission judder** — TSB fix is fluid + flash. Don't assume transmission is failing.
- **Using power steering fluid** — R18 has electric PS. No fluid reservoir.
