# Nissan KA24DE

## Overview
The Nissan KA24DE is a 2.4 liter dual overhead cam inline-4 produced from 1997 to 2004 in the Nissan Frontier, Xterra, Altima, and 240SX (some markets). It replaced the KA24E SOHC with a more modern DOHC head featuring four valves per cylinder. Timing chain driven with dual chains (one primary, one balance shaft). Notably over-square bore/stroke (89 mm × 96 mm) gives good high-RPM breathing for a truck engine. All-aluminum head on cast iron block. Used in rear-wheel-drive (Frontier, 240SX) and front-wheel-drive (Altima) configurations.

## Known Issues
- **Timing chain rattle (cold start)** — Primary timing chain stretches around 150,000 miles. Guides wear, tensioner ratchet slips. Rattle lasts 2–3 seconds after cold start. Ignore it and the chain jumps time, bending valves (interference engine).
- **Balance shaft chain noise** — Separate smaller chain behind primary chain. Wears independently. Noise from lower front cover. Replace balance shaft chain when doing primary chain.
- **Valve cover gasket leak (spark plug tube seals)** — The gasket includes rubber spark plug tube seals that harden. Oil fills the plug wells, causing misfire and ignition coil damage.
- **Timing chain tensioner oil pressure failure** — Tensioner is hydraulically actuated. Low oil pressure (worn pump or wrong viscosity oil) = slack chain = rattle.
- **Crankshaft position sensor (CKP) failure** — Located in bell housing, reads flywheel teeth. Difficult to replace (transmission access or patience with 1/4-inch extensions).
- **Throttle body carbon buildup** — Drive-by-wire throttle plates gum up, causing idle droop and stall when AC engages.

## Common Procedures

### Timing Chain Replacement
1. Set to TDC #1 compression. Remove all accessories, harmonic balancer (105 ft-lb), and front cover.
2. **WARNING: Interference engine.** Do NOT rotate cams or crank independently with belt/chain removed.
3. Inspect primary chain for elongation (measure 16-link span; exceeds spec = replace).
4. Remove chain, guides, and tensioner. Inspect crank sprocket for hooked teeth.
5. Check balance shaft chain and guides. Replace simultaneously.
6. Install new chain with colored links aligned to cam and crank timing marks.
7. Install new tensioner, release ratchet, verify chain tension.
8. Hand-turn crank two revolutions, recheck timing. Must align perfectly.
9. Reassemble front cover. RTV at oil pan junction and cylinder head junction.

### Spark Plug Tube Seal Replacement
1. Remove ignition coils. If oil is visible in plug tubes, seals are leaking.
2. Remove valve cover. Old gasket includes integrated tube seals.
3. Clean head surface. Install new valve cover gasket kit with fresh tube seals.
4. Verify each tube is oil-free with compressed air before reinstalling coils.
5. Install coils with dielectric grease on boots.

## Special Tools
- Harmonic balancer puller/installer
- Timing chain wedge (holds tensioner compressed during install)
- 14 mm thin-wall spark plug socket (deep plug well)
- Torque wrench with low range (5 ft-lb for valve cover, 6 ft-lb for oil pan)

## Common Mistakes
- **Reusing old chain with new tensioner** — Stretched chain defeats new tensioner. Always replace chain, guides, and tensioner as a set.
- **Overtightening oil pan or valve cover** — Aluminum threads strip easily. 6 ft-lb feels barely snug.
- **Wrong transmission fluid** — Nissan Matic fluids are specific. Dexron in RE4R01A causes shift shock.
- **Ignoring balance shaft chain** — Behind primary chain, hidden, forgotten. Its failure causes vibration and stress on main chain.
