# Toyota 5VZ-FE

## Overview
The Toyota 5VZ-FE is a 3.4 liter V6 produced from 1995 to 2004 in the Toyota Tacoma, 4Runner, Tundra, and T100. Replaced the 3VZ-E (troubled early V6) with a thoroughly redesigned all-aluminum 60-degree V6. Dual overhead cam, 24 valves, timing belt, non-interference design. Features Toyota's ACIS (Acoustic Control Induction System) — a dual-stage intake manifold with a switching valve that opens a secondary resonance chamber at 3,900 RPM for a torque bump. Reliable, torque-rich, and understressed. The 5VZ is the engine that redeemed Toyota V6 reputation after the 3VZ.

## Known Issues
- **Valve cover gasket leak** — Rubber grommets for valve cover bolts harden, allowing oil seepage onto exhaust manifolds. Common on both banks.
- **Timing belt idler bearing noise** — Idler bearing develops a whine by 100k miles. Replace with belt every 90k miles.
- **ACIS intake manifold actuator failure** — The vacuum diaphragm that switches the intake runner fails. Stuck in short-runner mode = lost low-end torque. Stuck in long-runner = restricted top end. Diaphragm can be tested with handheld vacuum pump.
- **EGR pipe carbon clogging** — Same as 1MZ: the metal EGR pipe from exhaust to intake clogs with carbon, causing P0401.
- **Coolant bypass pipe corrosion** — A small steel pipe behind the water pump routes coolant to the heater core. Corrodes at O-rings, causing coolant seep into valley.
- **Knock sensor failure (under intake)** — Single knock sensor mounted under the intake manifold. Labor-intensive replacement. Sets P0325.
- **Oil pump seal leak** — Rear main seal and oil pump seal share the same space at the timing cover/rear main junction. Oil drip from bell housing area.
- **IAC valve carbon buildup** — Idle hunts or stalls. Remove and clean IAC pintle and bore.

## Common Procedures

### Timing Belt Replacement (Tacoma / 4Runner)
1. Remove radiator for access (Tacoma). Remove passenger-side engine mount (4Runner).
2. Remove accessory belts, crank pulley (181 ft-lb), timing covers.
3. **NOTE: Non-interference engine.** If belt breaks, valves do NOT hit pistons. But replace preventively.
4. Remove old belt. Inspect idler bearing — spin by hand. Any grittiness or noise = replace.
5. Set timing marks: crank dot at 12 o'clock. Cam sprocket marks at 12 o'clock.
6. Install new tensioner (always replace). Set pin to retracted position.
7. Install new belt. Release tensioner.
8. Hand-turn crank two revolutions. Recheck marks.
9. Torque crank pulley to 181 ft-lb. Reassemble.

### ACIS Intake Manifold Actuator Test
1. Locate actuator on intake manifold (passenger side).
2. Connect handheld vacuum pump to actuator nipple.
3. Apply 15 inHg vacuum. You should hear the runner valve "thunk" as it switches.
4. If no movement or diaphragm won't hold vacuum: replace actuator.
5. If actuator is OK: check vacuum supply line from intake manifold to actuator.

## Common Mistakes
- **Reusing timing belt idler** — Non-interference doesn't mean you want a roadside breakdown. Replace idler with belt.
- **Wrong ATF** — A340F requires Toyota Type T-IV. Dexron causes shift shock.
- **Overtorquing valve cover** — 7 ft-lb is barely more than hand-tight. Aluminum cracks easily.
- **Ignoring ACIS failure** — Owner complains of "no power." Quick vacuum test diagnoses it in 2 minutes.
- **Mixing Toyota Red and Pink coolant** — Flush completely. Mixing creates gel that clogs radiator.
- **Not checking coolant bypass pipe** — Hidden behind water pump. Leak drips into bell housing, mimicking rear main seal.
