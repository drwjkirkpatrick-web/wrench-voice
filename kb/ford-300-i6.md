# Ford 300 Inline-6

## Overview
The Ford 300 cubic inch (4.9 liter) inline-6 was manufactured from 1965 to 1996 in Ford F-Series trucks, E-Series vans, and Broncos. It is a cast iron block and head, pushrod OHV design with seven main bearings — one of the smoothest, longest-lasting gasoline engines ever built. Carbureted in early years, fuel-injected (EFI) from 1987 onward with a port-injection intake manifold. No timing belt — gear-driven camshaft off the crank via helical gears. Often called the "bulletproof six."

## Known Issues
- **Plastic timing gear wear at 150,000+ miles** — Early EFI engines used a plastic cam gear (steel crank gear, plastic cam gear). The teeth strip gradually, causing rough running that progresses to no-start. Replace with steel billet gear.
- **Distributor shaft bushing wobble** — Upper bushing wears, causing erratic timing and random misfires. Shaft has visible lateral play when removed.
- **EGR valve carbon jamming** — EFI engines have an EGR valve that sticks with carbon buildup, causing stumble and MIL illumination.
- **Thermostat housing corrosion** — Aluminum housing on EFI engines pits where it meets the iron block, causing chronic coolant seep.
- **Oil pan rust-through** — Pan is unprotected steel exposed to road grime. Rust holes develop around 15+ years in salt climates.
- **Motor mount collapse** — Right-side mount (near exhaust) cooks and collapses. Engine drops slightly, shifter linkage binds.

## Common Procedures

### Timing Gear Replacement
1. Drain coolant, remove radiator, fan, and accessory belts.
2. Remove harmonic balancer (90 ft-lb, impact recommended).
3. Remove timing cover. Note the oil slinger behind balancer.
4. Inspect cam gear teeth. Hooked, chipped, or worn valleys = replace.
5. Critical: The cam gear is a PRESS FIT on the camshaft. Requires a gear puller and installer tool (or careful torch heating + rapid installation).
6. Use a steel billet replacement gear — DO NOT reinstall plastic.
7. Check crank gear condition. If damaged, remove with puller and replace.
8. Apply assembly lube to gears, install cover with RTV, reinstall balancer.

### Oil Pan Replacement
1. Jack vehicle securely, support on stands. Remove front differential if 4x4.
2. Remove starter for access.
3. Remove all pan bolts. Expect some to snap — replace with hex-head bolts.
4. Clean gasket surfaces thoroughly. Block surface must be bare metal.
5. Use cork-rubber gasket with aviation Permatex on both sides. Do NOT use RTV alone — it squeezes out.
6. Torque in spiral pattern to 10 ft-lb MAX. Overtightening strips block threads permanently.
7. Refill oil, start, check for leaks at RTV joints.

## Special Tools
- Cam gear installer + puller set (Ford OTC 303-290 or equivalent)
- 1-1/4 inch socket for harmonic balancer bolt
- Magnetic pickup tool — you'll drop at least one bolt into the bell housing
- 15 mm crows foot for exhaust manifold flange nuts

## Common Mistakes
- **Reinstalling the plastic timing gear** — It WILL fail again. Steel billet is the permanent fix.
- **Overtightening oil pan bolts** — Stripped threads require helicoil inserts. 10 ft-lb feels surprisingly loose.
- **Ignoring the oil slinger** — The thin washer behind the harmonic balancer must be reinstalled. Without it, front seal leaks.
- **Not indexing the distributor** — Remove distributor carefully noting rotor position. Reinstall with rotor pointed at #1 plug wire. Off by one tooth = hard start or backfire.
