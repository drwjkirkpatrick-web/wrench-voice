# Toyota 2GR-FE

## Overview
The Toyota 2GR-FE is a 3.5 liter V6 produced from 2004 to present in the Toyota Camry, Avalon, Highlander, RAV4, Sienna, Venza, and Lexus ES350/RX350/GS350. All-aluminum 60-degree V6 with dual overhead cams, 24 valves, and Toyota's Dual VVT-i (variable timing on both intake and exhaust cams). Chain-driven (no timing belt). 268–306 hp depending on application and year. Port injection on early engines; direct + port injection (D-4S) on later variants (2GR-FSE, 2GR-FXS). One of Toyota's most prolific modern engines — millions produced, remarkably few issues for a complex dual-VVT engine.

## Known Issues
- **Water pump failure (2004–2010)** — Early 2GR water pumps have a composite impeller that separates from the shaft. Coolant circulation stops, engine overheats rapidly. Aftermarket pumps with metal impellers solve this permanently.
- **Timing chain tensioner rattle (cold start, high mileage)** — Chain tensioner uses oil pressure to maintain tension. Worn tensioner bleeds down overnight, causing 1–2 second rattle at startup. Not immediately critical but replace before it skips.
- **VVT-i actuator seal leak (both banks)** — The camshaft actuator seals harden and leak oil onto timing chain cover. Oil-soaked chain accelerates wear.
- **Carbon buildup on intake valves (direct injection variants)** — 2GR-FSE and D-4S engines (GS350, IS350) have no fuel washing intake valves. Walnut blasting every 60k–100k miles restores performance.
- **Spark plug thread damage in aluminum head** — Over-torqued plugs strip threads easily. Time-Sert or Helicoil repair required.
- **Serpentine belt tensioner failure** — Tensioner bearing seizes, belt squeals or throws. Replace belt and tensioner together.
- **Oil filter housing O-ring leak ( Lexus variants)** — Same M54-style leak pattern: oil filter housing gasket hardens, oil drips onto alternator and power steering pump.
- **Camshaft position sensor oil contamination** — Leaking actuator seals fill sensor connectors with oil, causing erratic VVT-i operation and P0011/P0021.
- **PCV valve clogging** — Positive crankcase valve sticks, causing excessive crankcase pressure. Blows out oil seals.

## Common Procedures

### Timing Chain Tensioner Replacement (Front Cover Removal)
1. This is a MAJOR job on the 2GR. The timing chains are at the FRONT of the engine, buried under accessories.
2. Remove hood for access. Support engine. Remove all accessories, alternator, PS pump, A/C compressor.
3. Remove valve covers (silicone gaskets, reusable if intact).
4. Remove crank pulley (184 ft-lb — requires SST holder + impact or long breaker).
5. Remove timing chain cover. Large RTV-mated cover. Expect old RTV to be stubborn.
6. Inspect chains, guides, tensioners. Both banks have separate chains.
7. Replace tensioner and any worn guides. Chains rarely stretch but inspect for elongation.
8. Install new cover gasket with Toyota FIPG (Formed-In-Place Gasket) sealant.
9. Torque crank pulley to 184 ft-lb. Reassemble.

### Water Pump Replacement
1. Drain coolant. Remove serpentine belt.
2. Remove alternator and bracket for access (varies by year).
3. Remove water pump bolts. Pump is mounted to timing cover.
4. Clean gasket surface thoroughly. Do NOT gouge timing cover.
5. Install new pump (prefer aftermarket with METAL impeller).
6. Use new O-ring or gasket per pump instructions.
7. Refill with Toyota Pink coolant. Burp thoroughly — 2GR has bleed screws on heater hoses.

### D-4S Carbon Cleaning (Direct Injection)
1. Remove intake manifold. Cover all ports with clean rags.
2. Hand-turn crank so intake valves are CLOSED on one bank.
3. Walnut blast intake ports using dedicated media blaster (fine crushed walnut shells).
4. Suction out walnut debris with vacuum.
5. Rotate crank to close valves on other bank. Repeat.
6. Reinstall manifold with new gaskets.
7. Clear adaptations. Drive 20 miles to relearn fuel trims.

- Thread repair kit (Time-Sert or Helicoil M14x1.25 for spark plugs)

## Common Mistakes
- **Wrong coolant** — Toyota Pink Super Long Life only. Mixed coolant types create sludge and clog heater cores.
- **Wrong ATF** — U-series requires Toyota WS. Dexron or Type T-IV causes harsh shifts.
- **Reusing silicone valve cover gaskets** — They CAN be reused if intact and not torn. If oil leaks, replace.
- **Not checking both banks for VVT-i leaks** — Both intake and exhaust actuators have seals. Replace all at once.
- **Overtorquing plugs in aluminum head** — 15 ft-lb is finger-tight on a torque wrench. Easy to strip.
- **Skipping walnut blast on D-4S** — By 80k miles, intake valves are caked. Power loss is gradual — owner doesn't notice until cleaned.
- **Cheap water pump with composite impeller** — Buy once, cry once. Metal impeller pumps are worth the premium.
