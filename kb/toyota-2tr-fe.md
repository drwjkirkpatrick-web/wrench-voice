# Toyota 2TR-FE

## Overview
The Toyota 2TR-FE is a 2.7 liter inline-4 produced from 2004 to present in the Toyota Tacoma, 4Runner, Hilux, and Fortuner. Replaced the 3RZ-FE with a modern dual-VVT-i design, all-aluminum construction, and chain-driven valvetrain. 16-valve DOHC. Outputs 159 hp and 180 lb-ft — modest numbers but extremely torque-rich at low RPM, ideal for truck applications. Known for being nearly unkillable with basic maintenance. Timing chain eliminates belt service. Dual VVT-i provides broad torque curve.

## Known Issues
- **Timing chain rattle at cold start (high mileage)** — Chain tensioner bleeds down overnight. One-second rattle on startup. Replace tensioner before it skips.
- **Oil filter housing seal leak** — Similar to 2GR: the oil filter bracket O-ring hardens, dripping oil onto exhaust manifold.
- **Water pump weep hole seepage** — Ceramic seal weeps coolant. Visible as white crust below pump.
- **Secondary air injection system failure (California models)** — AIR pump, valves, and plumbing fail, setting P0418. Common in coastal/rustbelt areas. Many owners bypass with block-off plates (emissions-illegal but functional).
- **Carbon buildup on intake valves** — Port injection only (no direct injection), so less severe than D-4S. Still accumulates slowly. Walnut blast every 120k miles restores efficiency.
- **Ignition coil failure ( humid climates)** — Coil-on-plog degradation at 120k+ miles. Misfire under load, especially morning dew.
- **Throttle body carbon buildup** — Drive-by-wire plate gums up. Idle droop. Clean with throttle body cleaner.
- **PCV valve clogging** — Sticks open, causing oil consumption and fouled plugs. Replace every 60k miles.

## Common Procedures

### Timing Chain Tensioner Replacement
1. Remove valve cover. Remove timing chain cover (front of engine).
2. Requires removing harmonic balancer (159 ft-lb — impact or SST holder).
3. Inspect chain guides. Replace if worn or cracked.
4. Remove old tensioner. Install new tensioner, prime by pushing plunger.
5. Release tensioner. Verify chain rides correctly on guides.
6. Hand-turn crank two revolutions. Recheck tension.
7. Install cover with fresh RTV. Torque balancer to 159 ft-lb.

### Secondary Air Injection Bypass (California/emissions troubleshooting)
1. **LEGAL NOTE:** This is for OFF-ROAD or RACE USE ONLY. Street use violates federal emissions law.
2. Locate AIR pump (passenger side, below intake manifold).
3. Remove pump, hoses, and check valves.
4. Install block-off plates on cylinder head ports.
5. Delete AIR-related fuses. Use OBD-II bypass module or tune to suppress P0418.

## Common Mistakes
- **Wrong oil weight** — 0W-20 is specified for CAFE. In hot climates or towing, 5W-30 is acceptable.
- **Reusing timing chain tensioner** — The rattle is a warning. Skip it and the chain may skip, causing VVT-i codes.
- **Ignoring AIR pump codes** — P0418 doesn't affect drivability but sets CEL. If not in emissions-testing state, many owners ignore it.
- **Overtorquing drain plug** — Aluminum pan threads strip. 27 ft-lb is enough.
