# Honda H-Series

## Overview
The Honda H-series is a 2.2–2.3 liter inline-4 produced from 1992 to 2001 in the Honda Prelude (BB1–BB9) and Accord (rare). Two main variants: H22A (2.2L, 190–200 hp, JDM) and H23A (2.3L, 160 hp, non-VTEC). The H22A is legendary for being Honda's highest-output naturally aspirated 4-cylinder until the K20: 200 hp from 2.2 liters via DOHC VTEC, roller rocker arms, and a tuned intake manifold. The Prelude Type SH added ATTS (Active Torque Transfer System) which complicates drivetrain work. All H-series are interference engines with timing belts.

## Known Issues
- **Oil pump seal leak / balance shaft seal leak** — Same failure mode as F-series: balance shaft seal behind timing belt cover leaks oil onto belt. Mandatory replacement with every timing belt.
- **Oil consumption (H22A4, 1997–2001)** — The H22A4 developed oil consumption issues via worn valve stem seals and/or piston ring blow-by. Use high-mileage synthetic oil, check every 1,000 miles.
- **VTEC solenoid screen clogging** — Oil screen on VTEC solenoid traps debris. Symptoms: VTEC doesn't engage above 5,800 RPM, engine feels flat. Remove solenoid, clean screen with brake cleaner.
- **Timing belt tensioner failure** — Hydraulic tensioner leaks down. Rattle at startup on interference engine = potential catastrophe. Replace with belt.
- **ATTS fluid contamination (Prelude Type SH)** — The Active Torque Transfer System shares ATF with the transmission. Contaminated ATF damages the ATTS clutch packs. Use Honda ATF-Z1 only.
- **H22A head warpage after overheating** — The aluminum head warps easily if overheated. Warp tolerance is very tight. Any overheating event requires a deck-resurface at machine shop.
- **Idle Air Control valve failure** — Same as D/F series: carbon buildup causes hunting idle. Clean with TB cleaner.
- **Distributor internal bearing failure** — The distributor shaft bearing wears, causing erratic ignition timing and misfire. Replace entire distributor assembly.
- **Clutch master cylinder failure (manual)** — Pedal sinks to floor, soft feel. Common on high-mileage Preludes. Replace master and slave together.

## Common Procedures

### VTEC Solenoid Screen Cleaning
1. Remove valve cover. Locate VTEC solenoid on intake side, near front.
2. Remove 10mm bolts. Carefully pull solenoid.
3. The oil screen is a tiny mesh cylinder that sits in the solenoid port. It often falls out.
4. Soak in brake cleaner. Blow out with compressed air.
5. Check solenoid resistance: 14–30 Ω at room temp.
6. Reinstall with new O-ring. Clear adaptations.
7. Test: rev past 5,800 RPM in 2nd gear. Should feel distinct VTEC surge.

### Timing Belt Replacement (Interference — CRITICAL)
1. **WARNING: INTERFERENCE ENGINE.** If timing belt breaks or jumps, pistons WILL contact valves.
2. Set TDC #1. Remove crank pulley (181 ft-lb), accessory belts, covers.
3. Remove old belt. Inspect EVERYTHING: cam seals, balance shaft seal, water pump, idler.
4. **ALWAYS replace balance shaft seal with belt.** It's $5 and prevents oil contamination.
5. Check water pump for play. Spin by hand — should be silent and smooth.
6. Install new belt with Honda marks aligned: crank dot at 12 o'clock, both cam sprocket "UP" marks at 12 o'clock.
7. Install new tensioner (hydraulic type). Set pin to retracted position.
8. Release tensioner. Hand-turn crank TWO full revolutions. Recheck alignment.
9. If any mark is off by even one tooth: remove belt, re-align, reinstall.
10. Torque crank pulley to 181 ft-lb.

### ATTS Service (Prelude Type SH)
1. The ATTS unit is bolted to the front of the transmission.
2. Drain ATF from transmission (includes ATTS).
3. Refill with Honda ATF-Z1 to proper level.
4. Test ATTS function: tight circles in parking lot. Inner wheel should accelerate faster than outer.
5. If ATTS is inoperative: check ATTS control unit (behind glovebox) and wheel speed sensors.

## Common Mistakes
- **Not replacing balance shaft seal** — The #1 cause of repeat timing belt failure on H-series. Oil destroys the belt.
- **Reusing timing belt tensioner** — Hydraulic tensioners lose pressure. Interference engine = catastrophic failure.
- **Wrong ATF in ATTS** — Generic ATF destroys ATTS clutch packs. Honda ATF-Z1 only.
- **Ignoring oil consumption** — H22A4 engines consume oil via valve seals. Low oil = spun bearing. Check weekly.
- **Not decking the head after overheat** — H22 heads warp easily. Even slight warp = head gasket repeat failure.
- **Wrong spark plugs** — Needs platinum or iridium. Copper fouls in DOHC VTEC combustion chambers.
