# Toyota 1UZ-FE / 2UZ-FE

## Overview
The Toyota 1UZ-FE (4.0L V8, 1989–2004) and 2UZ-FE (4.7L V8, 1998–2009) are Toyota's first and second-generation V8 engines. The 1UZ powered the Lexus LS400 and SC400, establishing Toyota's reputation for V8 refinement. All-aluminum 90-degree V8, quad cam (DOHC on both banks), 32 valves, timing belt on 1UZ (non-interference), chain on 2UZ (also non-interference). The 2UZ is a bored/stroked evolution with iron liners in the block for truck duty (Land Cruiser, Tundra, Sequoia, LX470). Both are absurdly overbuilt — 1UZ engines routinely exceed 400,000 miles with basic maintenance.

## 1UZ-FE Known Issues
- **Timing belt idler bearing seizure** — Smooth idler bearing seizes at 120k+ miles, throwing the belt. Non-interference means no valve damage, but engine stalls. Replace idler with belt.
- **Starter solenoid contacts** — Same as 22RE: copper contacts erode, requiring multiple key turns. Rebuild kit available.
- **Knock sensor failure (rear bank)** — Inaccessible under intake manifold. Sets P0325. Labor-intensive replacement.
- **Oil cooler line corrosion (LS400)** — Same crimp-fitting corrosion as 1MZ. Oil spray onto exhaust.
- **ECU capacitor leakage (1989–1994)** — Age causes electrolytic capacitors in ECU to leak, corroding traces. Symptoms: erratic idle, random stalling. Requires ECU rebuild.
- **Valve cover gasket leak** — Oil drips onto exhaust manifolds from both banks. Common at 150k+ miles.
- **Power steering pump leak** — Pump seal weeps onto alternator, causing charging issues.

## 2UZ-FE Known Issues
- **Timing chain rattle (cold start, high mileage)** — Chain tensioner bleeds down overnight. Brief rattle at startup. Non-interference but replace preventively.
- **Exhaust manifold cracking** — Cast iron manifolds crack between ports. Common on Land Cruiser and Tundra. Ticking sound at cold start.
- **Water pump composite impeller (early 2UZ)** — Same failure mode as early 2GR: impeller separates from shaft. Use metal-impeller replacement.
- **Knock sensor failure** — Located under intake, between cylinder banks. Nearly impossible without removing intake manifold.
- **Secondary air injection pump failure (California)** — AIR pump fails, setting P0418. Common in rustbelt/coastal areas.
- **Oil leak from front crank seal** — Seal hardens, oil sprays onto serpentine belt. Belt slips, accessories undercharge.
- **VVT-i actuator seal leak (2005+ 2UZ)** — Later engines with VVT-i develop same actuator seal leaks as 2GR.

## Common Procedures

### 1UZ Timing Belt Replacement
1. Remove hood for access. Remove passenger-side engine mount.
2. Remove crank pulley (181 ft-lb), accessory belts, covers.
3. Set timing marks: crank dot at 12 o'clock. Both cam sprockets have marks at 12 o'clock.
4. Remove old belt. Inspect idler bearing.
5. Install new tensioner. Release pin.
6. Install new belt. Hand-turn crank two revolutions. Recheck marks.
7. Torque crank pulley to 181 ft-lb.

### 2UZ Timing Chain Tensioner Replacement
1. Remove valve covers. Remove timing chain cover (front).
2. Requires harmonic balancer removal (184 ft-lb).
3. Remove old tensioner. Install new, prime by pushing plunger.
4. Hand-turn crank. Recheck chain tension.
5. Reassemble with fresh RTV on cover.

### Exhaust Manifold Crack Repair (2UZ)
1. Inspect manifold for hairline cracks (common between ports).
2. Small cracks: weld repair possible (cast iron welding requires preheat).
3. Large/multiple cracks: replace manifold + gasket + studs.
4. Apply anti-seize to new studs.

## Special Tools
- Toyota SST 09213-70010 (crankshaft holder)
- Torque angle gauge
- Long 3/8 extension (rear bank spark plugs)
- Thread chaser for exhaust studs (common corrosion)

## Common Mistakes
- **Reusing timing belt idler on 1UZ** — Non-interference doesn't mean you want a stall on the highway.
- **Ignoring oil cooler lines** — Same $80 fix prevents engine fire.
- **Not checking ECU capacitors on early 1UZ** — A $40 capacitor kit prevents a $1,000 ECU replacement.
- **Wrong ATF** — Both require Toyota Type T-IV.
- **Overtorquing valve cover on 2UZ** — Same 7 ft-lb limit. Easy to crack.
