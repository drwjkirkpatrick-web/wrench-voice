# Toyota 7M-GTE / 7M-GE

## Overview
The Toyota 7M is a 3.0 liter inline-6 produced from 1986 to 1992 in the Toyota Supra (MKIII) and Toyota Cressida. Two variants: 7M-GE (naturally aspirated, 200 hp) and 7M-GTE (turbocharged, 230 hp). Iron block, aluminum head, single overhead cam with 24 valves, timing belt (interference design). The 7M-GTE was Toyota's first production turbo inline-6 and established the template later refined by the 1JZ and 2JZ. Despite its SOHC design, the 7M produces excellent torque and responds well to boost. The 7M head gasket is notorious — one of the most discussed failures in Toyota history.

## Known Issues
- **HEAD GASKET FAILURE — THE 7M'S DEFINING ISSUE** — Toyota undertorqued the head bolts from the factory. Head bolts relax over time, head lifts slightly, gasket fails between cylinders 3–4 and 4–5. Coolant enters combustion chamber. White smoke, overheating, coolant in oil. The fix: ARP head studs torqued to 80 ft-lb (not the factory 56 ft-lb). This is not optional on ANY 7M.
- **Turbocharger oil starvation (GTE)** — CT26 turbo oil feed line carbonizes. Restricted flow = bearing failure = smoke. Upgrade to braided line.
- **Crankshaft thrust bearing wear (manual transmission)** — The R154 manual transmission transmits axial load through crank thrust bearings. Clutch dumps and hard launches cause thrust bearing failure. Check crank endplay.
- **Ignition coil / igniter failure** — Same igniter weakness as 3S-GTE. External module overheats. Carry spare.
- **Idle speed control valve (ISC) clogging** — Gums up with oil vapor. Idle hunts. Clean with carb cleaner.
- **Water pump seal failure** — Ceramic seal weeps. Common at 100k+ miles.
- **Timing belt idler bearing** — Seizes, throwing belt on interference engine = valve damage. Replace with belt.
- **Fuel injector seals hardening** — Leak fuel into intake, causing rich condition and hydrolock risk.
- **Boost leak from factory intercooler couplers** — Rubber couplers crack. Replace with silicone.

## Common Procedures

### THE Head Gasket Fix (Mandatory on Every 7M)
1. **This is the single most important repair on any 7M.**
2. Symptoms: white smoke, coolant loss, overheating, coolant in oil, compression test showing adjacent cylinders leaking.
3. Remove cylinder head. Send to machine shop for flatness check (must be < 0.003" warp).
4. Clean block deck thoroughly. Chase threads.
5. Install ARP head studs with ARP Ultra-Torque lube on threads and under washers.
6. Torque in sequence: 40 → 60 → 80 ft-lb. THIS IS HIGHER THAN FACTORY SPEC. Toyota's 56 ft-lb is the root cause of the epidemic.
7. Use Cometic or HKS MLS head gasket.
8. Reassemble. After first heat cycle, retorque ARP studs (back off 1/4 turn, retorque to 80 ft-lb).
9. Break in gently for 500 miles.

### Turbo Oil Feed Line Upgrade
1. Remove stock hard line from block to CT26.
2. Inspect for carbon buildup (restricted flow).
3. Install braided stainless -4AN line with 0.06" restrictor.
4. Route away from exhaust heat with heat sleeve.

## Common Mistakes
- **Using factory head bolt torque** — This is the original sin. 56 ft-lb kills every 7M eventually. Use ARP studs at 80 ft-lb.
- **Ignoring head gasket warning signs** — White smoke = stop driving. Continued operation destroys bearings with coolant in oil.
- **Wrong oil for turbo** — 5W-30 shears under turbo heat. Use 10W-40 synthetic minimum.
- **Reusing stock head bolts** — They stretch. ARP studs are reusable.
- **Not retorquing ARP studs after heat cycle** — ARP requires this step. Skipping it = gradual loosening.
- **Cheap head gasket** — OEM or Cometic MLS only. Cheap gaskets fail again within 20k miles.
