# Toyota 3S-GTE

## Overview
The Toyota 3S-GTE is a 2.0 liter turbocharged inline-4 produced from 1986 to 2007 in the Toyota Celica GT-Four, MR2 Turbo, and Caldina GT-T. Four generations: Rev 1 (1986–1989, CT26 turbo, 182 hp), Rev 2 (1990–1993, updated head, 200 hp), Rev 3 (1994–1999, CT20B turbo, 242–255 hp), and Rev 4 (2002–2007, Caldina only, 260 hp). Iron block, aluminum head, DOHC, 16 valves, timing belt (interference). The 3S-GTE powered Toyota's World Rally Championship cars and is legendary for its robustness under boost — stock internals reliably handle 300+ hp with proper tuning and fuel.

## Known Issues
- **Cylinder head lift under high boost** — The iron block expands differently than the aluminum head. Under sustained high boost, the head gasket fails between cylinders 2 and 3 (hottest area). ARP head studs and Cometic/ARP gasket required above 18 PSI.
- **Turbocharger oil starvation** — The CT26 oil feed line restricts with carbon. Starved bearings = seal failure = smoke. Upgrade to braided line.
- **Ignition amplifier (igniter) failure** — The external igniter module overheats and fails. Symptoms: sudden no-spark, engine dies. Often fails on hot days. Carry a spare.
- **MR2 Turbo overheating in traffic** — Mid-engine layout with limited airflow at idle. Stock cooling marginal. Upgrade to larger radiator + electric fans for track use.
- **Knock sensor failure / false knock** — Stock knock sensor is conservative. False knock under boost causes ECU to retard timing, losing power. Relocate sensor or upgrade.
- **Rev 3 CT20B turbo oil seal** — The CT20B (ball-bearing) has a fragile ceramic seal that fails with age. Rebuild or upgrade to Garrett/GT series.
- **Fuel pump insufficient above 300 whp** — Stock pump (Walbro 255 equivalent) maxes out. Upgrade to dual-pump or larger single.
- **Boost leak from intercooler piping** — Factory rubber couplers crack with heat cycles. Replace with silicone.
- **Idle speed control valve (ISC) clogging** — Gums up with blow-by oil. Idle hunts. Remove and clean with carb cleaner.
- **Crankshaft position sensor heat failure** — Located near exhaust manifold on MR2. Heat-soaked sensor fails, causing no-start when hot.

## Common Procedures

### Turbo Oil Feed Line Upgrade
1. Remove stock hard line from block to turbo.
2. Inspect line for carbon buildup (small internal diameter = restricted flow).
3. Install braided stainless -4AN line with restrictor (0.06 inch orifice).
4. **CRITICAL:** Do NOT use unrestricted line — too much oil overwhelms turbo seals.
5. Route line away from exhaust manifold heat. Use heat sleeve.

### Head Stud Installation (High Boost)
1. Drain coolant. Remove cylinder head.
2. Chase block threads with ARP thread chaser.
3. Install ARP 2000 or 625+ head studs with ARP Ultra-Torque lube.
4. Torque in sequence: 50 → 75 → 100 ft-lb (ARP spec for 2000 series).
5. Use Cometic MLS head gasket with proper bore diameter.
6. Reassemble. Retorque after first heat cycle.

### MR2 Turbo Cooling Upgrade
1. Remove stock radiator (driver side, mid-engine).
2. Install larger cross-flow radiator with dual SPAL fans.
3. Add oil cooler (thermostatically controlled, 180°F).
4. Consider water/methanol injection for track use.
5. **IMPORTANT:** Stock cooling is adequate for street. Upgrades needed ONLY for track/autocross.

## Special Tools
- ARP torque spec sheet (head studs differ from factory TTY)
- Torque angle gauge (for stock head bolts if not upgrading)
- Boost leak tester (pressurize intake to 20 PSI, listen/soap for leaks)
- Wideband O2 sensor (essential for tuning — stock narrowband is not sufficient)
- Igniter test light (diagnoses igniter vs coil failure)

## Common Mistakes
- **Running high boost on stock head bolts** — Head lift between 2 and 3 is guaranteed above 18 PSI. ARP studs are mandatory.
- **Neglecting turbo oil feed line** — The #1 killer of CT26/CT20B turbos. Braided line costs $40.
- **Wrong spark plug heat range** — Stock heat range on a 300 whp engine = detonation. Step 2–3 colder.
- **Not upgrading fuel system** — Stock injectors (440cc) max at ~280 whp. 550cc+ and pump upgrade needed.
- **False knock retard** — The ECU pulls timing aggressively. Relocate knock sensor or upgrade to less sensitive unit.
- **Ignoring intercooler couplers** — A $5 silicone coupler prevents a $2,000 turbo rebuild from overspeed.
- **Not carrying spare igniter** — On a GT-Four in the desert, a failed igniter strands you. $80 insurance policy.
