# Subaru EJ25 (SOHC)

## Overview
The Subaru EJ25 SOHC is a 2.5 liter flat-4 (boxer) engine produced from 1998 to 2010 in the Forester, Legacy, Outback, Impreza, and Baja. Single overhead cam per cylinder bank. Phase 1 (1998–1999) used 10mm head bolts; Phase 2 (2000–2010) used 11mm bolts with 14mm outer bolts. The EJ25 SOHC is infamous for external head gasket leaks — the most common Subaru failure mode in the shop.

## Known Issues
- **External head gasket leak** — Coolant weeps from head-to-block seam on cylinder 2 or 4. Oil stays clean. Leak point is visible from below with a flashlight. Prevalence: extremely high on 1998–2003 models.
- **Timing chain/belt rattle** — Actually a timing belt engine. Belt tensioner weakens at 100k+ miles. Replace every 105k miles.
- **Oil consumption** — Piston ring design on early Phase 2 engines consumes up to 1 qt per 1000 miles.
- **Rod knock from oil starvation** — Boxer layout + poor oil pickup design = bearing wear on cyl 2 and 4 under hard cornering.

## Common Procedures

### Head Gasket Replacement (External Leak)
1. **WARNING:** Use MLS (Multi-Layer Steel) gaskets only. Composite gaskets will fail again.
2. Remove intake manifold, exhaust manifolds, timing belt, camshafts.
3. Remove cylinder heads. Inspect block deck and head for flatness (< 0.002" warp).
4. Machine shop: resurface if needed. Do NOT remove more than 0.004".
5. Clean block threads — chase with tap. Subaru threads are fine and strip easily.
6. Install new MLS head gaskets (marked UP). Use Subaru OEM or Six-Star.
7. Torque in stages per factory sequence. Phase 1: 10mm bolts. Phase 2+: 11mm + 14mm.
8. Reassemble timing belt with new tensioner and water pump.
9. Refill with Subaru blue coolant. Burp thoroughly — heater on max, front end raised.

### Timing Belt Replacement
1. **WARNING:** ALL Subaru EJ engines are INTERFERENCE. Belt failure = bent valves.
2. Replace belt, tensioner, idler pulleys, and water pump as a kit.
3. Align crank mark to timing cover arrow.
4. Align cam sprocket dots to notches on backing plate.
5. Ensure belt is tight on non-tensioned side before releasing tensioner.
6. Rotate engine by hand two full revolutions. Recheck alignment.

## Special Tools
- 30 mm socket for crank bolt
- Strap wrench or chain wrench to hold harmonic balancer
- Crows foot for tight exhaust manifold nut near firewall
- Subaru cam sprocket holder tool (or equivalent)

## Common Mistakes
- **Using composite head gaskets** — MLS only. Composite will fail within 20k miles.
- **Not replacing timing belt components as a kit** — Tensioner or idler failure will destroy the new belt.
- **Skipping the burp** — Air pockets cause localized boiling, head warpage, repeat failure.
- **Reusing Phase 2 head bolts** — They are TTY-style and stretch. Replace.
