# Subaru FA20 (BRZ / 86 / FR-S)

## Overview
The Subaru FA20 is a 2.0 liter flat-4 (boxer) engine co-developed with Toyota, used in the Subaru BRZ, Toyota 86, and Scion FR-S from 2013–2020. Chain-driven DOHC with Toyota D-4S dual injection (port + direct). High-revving (7400 rpm redline). Open-deck aluminum block. Boxer layout keeps center of gravity low for sports car handling.

## Known Issues
- **Valve spring failure** — Early 2013 production had valve springs that could fracture. Recall campaign. Causes misfire, possible valve-to-piston contact.
- **RTV sealant in oil pickup** — Excess RTV from factory blocks oil pickup screen. Oil starvation = rod knock.
- **Carbon buildup on intake valves** — Direct injection side means no fuel washing intake valves. Walnut blast every 60k miles.
- **Camshaft carrier bolt leak** — Torque-to-yield bolts on cam carriers can weep oil. Must replace bolts when resealing.

## Common Procedures

### Valve Spring Replacement (Recall)
1. Remove cylinder head covers.
2. Compress valve springs one at a time using overhead valve spring compressor.
3. Inspect springs for cracks or deformation.
4. Replace with updated springs (recall part).
5. Verify valve-to-piston clearance if engine was cranked with broken spring.

### Oil Pickup Cleaning (RTV Prevention)
1. Drop oil pan.
2. Inspect pickup tube screen for RTV chunks or debris.
3. Clean thoroughly with brake cleaner.
4. Reinstall pickup with new O-ring.
5. Use MINIMUM RTV on pan gasket — thin bead only.

## Special Tools
- Valve spring compressor (overhead type)
- Camshaft holder tool for chain tension
- Torque angle gauge (TTY bolts)

## Common Mistakes
- **Using too much RTV on oil pan** — Chunks break off and clog pickup. Thin bead only.
- **Skipping walnut blast** — Carbon buildup causes rough idle and misfire. Not optional on DI engines.
- **Reusing TTY head bolts** — Must replace. Subaru/Toyota TTY bolts stretch on first torque.
